01

Brand Overview

Brand Essence: Parent voice.

What we are: The Parent Advisory Council at Nicholas Senn High School. A parent-led body that advises on how Title I family engagement funds are spent, co-develops school policy, and connects families with resources.

Tagline: Parent-led. For our kids.

Personality: Organized warmth. Institutional enough to be taken seriously. Warm enough to feel like community. We sound like parents talking to parents, not administrators issuing memos.

The Brand Relationship

Senn Parents uses the same italic collegiate lettering as Senn High School athletics. The S with the V-notch is unmistakably Senn. The warm gold "PARENTS" is what makes it ours. We belong to the school community. Parents lead here.

05

Voice and Tone

We sound like parents talking to parents. Direct, warm, organized. Never bureaucratic. Never condescending.

This is us

"All parents and guardians are welcome. You do not need to be a member to attend."
"Take 2 minutes to tell us what your family needs."
"The PAC is led by parents, for parents."
"We just elected new officers and we are building real parent-led governance at our school."

This is not us

"Per CPS Policy 801.3, eligible stakeholders are invited to participate in the advisory capacity..."
"Don't miss this AMAZING opportunity!!!"
"The administration has determined that parent input is valued..."

Voice Rules

  • Direct. Say what you mean. No jargon, no bureaucracy-speak.
  • Warm. We are neighbors, not an institution.
  • Inclusive. "All parents welcome" is not a tagline. It is a rule.
  • Bilingual. Key materials in English and Spanish.
  • Action-oriented. Every communication should tell parents what to do next.
06

Usage Rules

Do

  • Use the full lockup (SENN / PARENTS / Parent Advisory Council)
  • Maintain clear space around the logo
  • Use on white, black, or green backgrounds
  • Use the S avatar for profile pictures
  • Keep SENN in green and PARENTS in gold
  • Use Inter for all non-logo text

Don't

  • Stretch, rotate, or skew the logo
  • Change the colors
  • Add drop shadows, glows, or effects
  • Place on busy photo backgrounds
  • Use the Bulldog mascot (that is Athletics)
  • Recreate the lettering in a different font
