Context

Last year, the PAC collected approximately 14 survey responses. This year, at the March 18 Report Card Pick-Up, we collected over 100 in a single afternoon. Languages represented include English, Spanish, Kinyarwanda, and Swahili. Nearly every respondent provided their email and phone number, giving the PAC a real contact list for outreach.

What Drove the Results

The single biggest driver was coordinating with teachers and administrators stationed behind the report card tables. By briefing them ahead of time and asking them to direct parents to the PAC table, we turned every report card interaction into a touchpoint. Having the survey available on the website (sennparents.com) so parents could scan and complete it on their own phones also made a significant impact. A laptop was available at the table as a backup, but most parents preferred using their own devices. The lesson: peer coordination with staff beats any single tactic.

Title I Funding Context

Grant #430329 (Fund 332) Title I PAC funds are NOT subject to the April 8 general spending deadline. However, with the last day of school being June 4, 2026, all parent activities and training sessions should be scheduled throughout April and May.

Recommendations

What the Data Tells Us to Do

  • Schedule workshops in the evening. Work schedule is the #1 barrier (80%). Weekday evenings (4-8 PM) are the most accessible time for the majority of parents.
  • Always offer a virtual option. 30% of parents said virtual works best for their schedule. Use Zoom for hybrid access to every event.
  • Prioritize college and career planning. This was the #1 topic chosen by 75% of respondents. FAFSA and financial aid was also ranked highly (40%), especially among 11th and 12th grade parents.
  • Address mental health. The #2 priority at 65%. Partner with a CPS-approved wellness vendor like SEL Chicago, Wellness For Everyone, or Youth Guidance.
  • Run a Know Your Rights session. 40% selected this, particularly families who speak languages other than English. Bilingual facilitation is essential.
  • Invest in outreach and awareness. 45% had never heard of the PAC before this survey. 30% said "unaware of events" is a barrier. Email is the dominant channel (85%), but text and WhatsApp are important for multilingual families.
  • Build a community event. 45% want family arts, culture, and community events. This builds trust and brings in families who might not attend a formal workshop.
  • Plan for multilingual programming. Survey respondents speak English, Spanish, Kinyarwanda, Swahili, and other languages. Translation and culturally responsive facilitation are not optional.
